## 1. High-Intent, Low-Competition Numerology Topics — Coverage

### 1.1 What the research says

- **Low competition (0–25 difficulty), high intent:** Life path 1–9 and 11/22/33, **house number / home numerology**, life path calculator, name/expression calculator, karmic debt numbers.
- **Strategy:** Target specific numbers (life path, house number) and long-tail combos (e.g. “life path 5 numerology”) rather than broad “numerology” (high competition).
- **House number numerology** is repeatedly cited as very low competition (e.g. 0–4 difficulty, 8k–12k searches) and high intent (people moving, choosing homes, or curious about their address).

## 2. Psychic Pages to Promote PsychicOz (In Tune With Numerology)

### 2.1 Options

**Option A — Standalone “psychic + numerology” pages (no new hub)**  
Add a few focused pages that stay numerology-first but naturally lead to a reading:

- **“When to get a numerology reading”** — Life transitions, big decisions, recurring numbers you can’t interpret alone. CTA: PsychicOz (tarot or love/career by context).
- **“Numerology reading vs free calculator”** — When a live reading adds value (personalized, real-time, specific questions). CTA: PsychicOz.
- **“Questions to ask a numerology psychic”** — Love, career, timing, angel numbers. CTA: love/career/tarot by section.
- **“Angel numbers and psychic readings”** — How psychics interpret repeating numbers; when to get a reading. CTA: PsychicOz.

**Pros:** Fits current structure (e.g. under `/blog` or as static pages under a path like `/readings` or `/guides`). No new “category” in nav.  
**Cons:** No single “psychic readings” hub; discovery is via search or internal links.

**Option B — Psychic readings hub (new category)**  
Add a dedicated section:

- **Hub:** e.g. `/psychic-readings` or `/readings` — “Numerology & Psychic Readings”. Explains how numerology and psychic readings work together, when to get a reading, what to expect, links to PsychicOz (love / career / tarot).
- **Child pages (optional):** e.g. “Love readings”, “Career readings”, “Tarot & numerology”, “What to ask”, “First 3 minutes free”. Each page is SEO-friendly and ends with a clear CTA to the relevant PsychicOz offer.

**Pros:** One clear entry point; easy to add to nav (“Readings” or “Psychic Readings”); scales to more pages over time.  
**Cons:** New category to maintain; need to avoid duplicating blog/meaning content.

**Option C — Hybrid**  
- One **hub** page: `/psychic-readings` (or `/readings`) — overview, “numerology + readings”, when to get one, links to love/career/tarot.  
- A few **standalone** pages (e.g. “When to get a numerology reading”, “Questions to ask”) as blog posts or `/guides/...` that link to the hub and to PsychicOz.  
- No need (initially) for a full “category” of reading types; expand later if needed.

### 2.2 Recommendation

- **Short term:** Add **2–4 standalone pages** that are clearly “numerology + psychic” and link to PsychicOz (e.g. “When to get a numerology reading”, “Angel numbers and psychic readings”, “Questions to ask a numerology psychic”). Place them as **blog posts** or under a single path like **`/guides`** (e.g. `/guides/when-to-get-a-numerology-reading`) so we don’t over-formalize a new category yet.
- **If we want a clear “readings” entry point:** Add **one hub page** `/psychic-readings` (or `/readings`) that ties those guides together and links to PsychicOz love/career/tarot. We do **not** need a full new “category” of page types (many sub-routes) unless we plan to scale to 10+ reading-themed pages.
- **Content tone:** Keep pages **numerology-first** (what numerology is, when it helps, what questions to ask) and position a **live reading** as the next step when someone wants personalized, real-time answers. That keeps the site on-brand and supports PsychicOz without feeling like a separate “psychic site”.
